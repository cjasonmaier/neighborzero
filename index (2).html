The rule fits in one sentence

A tile clears when its neighbor count matches its number.

Every tile on the hex board shows a target. When the number of tiles touching it equals that target, it blooms and clears — and the board never stops reacting.

1

Swap

Tap any two cells — even an empty one — to swap their contents. Adjacency doesn't matter. One swap is one move.

2

Bloom

A tile whose occupied-neighbor count equals its target clears instantly. Watch for the numbers that are one neighbor away.

3

Cascade

Each bloom changes its neighbors' counts, which can set them off too — wave after wave, until the board falls quiet. Or falls empty.

What's inside

Deep enough to plan. Kind enough to finish.

No timers, no lives, no losing. Just you, the board, and the question of how few moves it can take.

A campaign of collections

Handcrafted levels grouped into collections of twelve, each introducing new wrinkles. Finish in fewer moves to earn up to three stars ★★★ per level.

A new puzzle every day

One fresh daily board, the same for everyone. Solve it, build your streak, and share your result without spoiling the solution.

Anchors and voids

Anchored tiles are locked in place but still bloom — plan around them. Voids aren't cells at all: nothing swaps in, and nothing counts them as a neighbor.

You can't lose

Clearing the board is the only outcome. Take as many moves as you need, restart freely, and grab a hint when a board has you cornered.
